The Eduba frame

A working view of the Invelon stack.

Roughly 60% of a new VR simulator build is traditional software work: Unity and Unreal scene assembly, asset pipelines, data model scaffolding, integration with Dynamics 365 Remote Assist or TeamViewer Frontline xPick. Another 30% is rule-based logic: branching scenarios, safety checks, procedural validation. Only the last 10% is genuine AI territory: LLM-driven dialogue, generative scenario authoring, instructor copilots that generate new content on demand.

Most XR studios today are either stuck in the 60% tier (shipping bespoke one-offs) or trying to force the 10% tier prematurely. The interesting work at Invelon's scale is the 30% layer: a rule-based authoring system, structured as folders rather than as a monolithic tool, that lets a junior engineer in Milan assemble a new scenario from existing modules. That is a methodology problem, not a framework problem.

The case

Correlation One with Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive.

The engagement worked because the enablement structure was designed as layered context that different roles could read at different depths. The same structural approach maps onto VR content authoring: scenario primitives, sector overlays, client-specific reskins, all readable by the engineering team and the training director without a handoff meeting.

1,500+ people trained since May 2025
6,000 to 9,000 hours saved per year
95% 30-day adoption rate

The map to Invelon is not perfect. Invelon trains through VR headsets while Correlation One trained through enterprise AI enablement. The relevance is that both are delivering workforce enablement at scale across regulated and multi-site clients, and the Correlation One engagement proves Eduba can build the orchestration around a training delivery partner rather than replacing them. This is the exact shape of a potential Invelon and Eduba referral for North American deployments.

The paper

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM).

ICM treats agent context as a layered filesystem, which is the same structural problem Invelon is quietly solving every time a new training scenario is built by cloning a previous one. If Invelon wants to move from bespoke-per-client to a composable authoring layer, ICM gives the folder-structure-as-architecture spine and a published methodology to cite in client pitches.
